Commodity Spotlight

Copper: The Metal Powering AI and Electrification

Copper has become one of the most important commodities in global markets. It is essential for power grids, electric vehicles, renewable energy infrastructure, and increasingly for AI data centers. Demand has surged as governments and companies invest heavily in electrification and digital infrastructure.

At the same time, mine supply remains constrained by operational disruptions and limited new projects, creating concerns about long-term shortages. Analysts continue to watch inventories, mine output, Chinese demand, and potential U.S. tariff actions as key drivers of copper prices.
